# Iceland · 4-Base Wilderness Itinerary
**Sept 10 – 29, 2026 · 19 nights · 4 Airbnbs · 4x4 SUV · Sony a7RV**

Land KEF **Sept 10, 9:25 AM** (YVR red-eye) → depart **Sept 29**. Clockwise route: South Coast → Eastfjords → North → Snæfellsnes, ending 2.5 h from the airport.

**Changes from the draft (both cut dead driving):**
- **Base 2 = Egilsstaðir**, not Seyðisfjörður — it's the hub every East hike radiates from; Seyðisfjörður becomes a day trip (~+50 min/day saved).
- **Base 3 = Eyjafjörður valley / Akureyri**, not Siglufjörður — Kerling trailhead 30 min instead of 1 h 15, Aldeyjarfoss 1 h 15 instead of 2 h 30; Siglufjörður becomes a day trip.
- **Base 4 (recommended) = Snæfellsnes (Grundarfjörður)** instead of the Westfjords — full Westfjords alternative preserved below.

**Crowd doctrine:** icons at sunrise or after 18:00; unmarked trails at midday; nobody is anywhere before 8 AM in September. New moon **Sept 11** (dark-sky aurora window, Bases 1–2), full moon **Sept 26**.

**Full route:** [Google Maps — KEF → Klaustur → Egilsstaðir → Akureyri → Grundarfjörður → KEF](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/Keflav%C3%ADk+International+Airport/Kirkjub%C3%A6jarklaustur/Egilssta%C3%B0ir/Akureyri/Grundarfj%C3%B6r%C3%B0ur/Keflav%C3%ADk+International+Airport)

## Logistics

**Food.** Big-shop at Bónus/Krónan/Nettó at every base handover; rural stores are sparse and pricey. Day 1: Krónan Selfoss (5 days of food — Klaustur has only a small Kjarval). Sept 15: Nettó/Bónus Egilsstaðir. Sept 20/21: Bónus Akureyri. Sept 25: Bónus Borgarnes (or Ísafjörður). Trail food: skyr, flatkökur, dried fish, chocolate, instant soup — pack lunch daily, trailhead cafés don't exist.

**Fuel.** Never below half a tank — East/Westfjords station gaps exceed 100 km. Get an N1 fob / Orkan app (unmanned pumps, PIN required). Critical fill-ups: Selfoss, Vík, Klaustur, Höfn, Egilsstaðir, Reykjahlíð, Akureyri, Borgarnes. Budget ≈ 2,900 km ≈ 260 L ≈ **80,000 ISK (~$570)**.

**Water & kit.** Tap water is glacier-grade everywhere — two 1.5 L bottles each, refill daily; thermos on summit days. Clear mountain streams drinkable, milky glacial water not. Hard-shell top+bottom, insulation, hat/gloves (Kerling/Helgrindur can be winter up top), light microspikes, headlamps for pre-dawn starts. Download offline Google Maps + AllTrails/Wikiloc tracks.

**Volcano watch (as of July 2026).** No active eruption — the last Sundhnúkur (Reykjanes) eruption ended Aug 2025, but magma is re-accumulating under Svartsengi and a new eruption is rated likely. The zone is 20 min from KEF and far from all bases (zero itinerary risk); if it erupts during the trip, it's a bolt-on for arrival/departure day. Check en.vedur.is + safetravel.is.

**Driving & safety.** 4x4 needed: Þakgil road, Aldeyjarfoss (F26 stretch), Stuðlagil east gravel, Kvennaskarð. Check **road.is** + **vedur.is** every morning; file a **safetravel.is** plan before Kerling/Helgrindur/Kaldbakur. Hold doors in wind (classic rental damage); never drive in a yellow/red warning; headlights always on.

**How to read it:** Bases 1–2 are the true dark-sky window — thin/absent moon = maximum aurora contrast and Milky Way frames (Jökulsárlón, Stuðlagil, Seyðisfjörður). From Base 3 on, the waxing moon lights the landscape itself: aurora still shows on camera, but switch to moonlit foregrounds (glaciers, falls, Kirkjufell in silver) at ISO 400–800.
- **Aurora:** new moon **Sept 11**, full moon **Sept 26** → darkest skies in Bases 1–2. Check vedur.is aurora + cloud map at 21:00 nightly; chase cloud gaps, not Kp. Pre-scouted foregrounds: Jökulsárlón (B1), Stuðlagil/Seyðisfjörður (B2), Goðafoss/Mývatn (B3), Kirkjufell/Búðakirkja (B4). Baseline: 14–20 mm, f/1.8–2.8, 5–10 s, ISO 1600–6400.
- **a7RV notes:** microfiber cloths ×3 + rain cover (spray at Skógafoss/Dettifoss), polarizer for moss/canyons, 6-stop ND for silky water, spare batteries warm, double cards.

**Weather-flex doctrine:** every base has a flex day; every hard hike a swap partner. Rank days by forecast, not by schedule — the plan is a menu, the sky is the boss.
