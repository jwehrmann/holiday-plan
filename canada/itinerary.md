# Canada · Rockies + Coast Mountains — Pre-Iceland Warm-Up

**Aug 14 – Sept 9, 2026 · 26 nights · 6 bases · rental SUV · Sony a7RV**

The month before Iceland, built to the same profile: big-view alpine trails, glaciers, and challenging non-technical **summit scrambles** (18 km / 1,000 m+ days are the norm). Arc runs **Vancouver → Golden (the Rockies hub) → back west through Revelstoke & the Coast Mountains → Vancouver**, landing you rested at YVR for the **Sept 9 red-eye to Keflavík** (lands Sept 10, 9:25 AM — the Iceland plan picks up from there).

**Flight assumption:** you're at **YVR for an evening departure Sept 9** (YVR→KEF red-eye). Vancouver is the deliberate safety base the night before — zero drive-day risk into the Iceland flight. If your out-date differs, the tail end re-tunes.

---

## Base logic & the big question you asked (where to stay after Golden)

- **Base 1 · Vancouver / Sea-to-Sky (Aug 14–17, 3 nights)** — arrival + jet-lag legs on the North Shore and in Squamish. **Save Garibaldi for the end** (it's a Whistler asset, not a Vancouver one).
- **Base 2 · Golden, BC (Aug 17–31, 14 nights)** — the anchor. Absurdly well-placed: **Yoho 45 min, Lake Louise 1 h, Rogers Pass/Glacier NP 1 h, Icefields Parkway 1.5–2.5 h, Canmore 2 h.** Optional **2–3-night Jasper side-trip** folded into this block (see below).
- **Base 3 · Revelstoke (Aug 31–Sept 2, 2 nights)** — the recommended first stop heading home: only **1 h 45 from Golden, directly on the way**, with Mount Revelstoke's alpine-meadow day hikes. Breaks up the long westward haul.
- **Base 4 · Kamloops (Sept 2–3, 1 transit night)** — Revelstoke→Whistler is 7–8 h; don't push it in one shot. A pure sleep-stop halfway.
- **Base 5 · Whistler / Squamish (Sept 3–8, 5 nights)** — where the biggest **Coast Mountains** days live: Garibaldi (Panorama Ridge / Black Tusk), Wedgemount, the Stawamus Chief, Whistler alpine. The trip's climax, hit at peak fitness.
- **Base 6 · Vancouver (Sept 8–9, 1 night)** — the pre-Iceland breather 30–40 min from YVR. Board the red-eye rested.

**Why this sequence:** it minimizes backtracking (Revelstoke is on-route; Garibaldi belongs to Whistler, not Vancouver), front-loads the long drive, and lands the marquee coast hikes last. **Alternative:** skip Kamloops and drive Revelstoke→Whistler in one 7–8 h day (gain a Whistler night); or base in **Squamish** instead of Whistler if you'd rather be 1 h from Vancouver and closer to the Chief (Garibaldi's Rubble Creek trailhead sits between the two).

## Base 2 · Golden, BC — the Rockies hub
**Mon Aug 17 – Mon Aug 31 · 14 nights.** One base, five worlds within 1.5 h. This is the meat of the trip. Below is the menu, grouped by area; rank days by weather and shuttle luck, not by schedule.

### Yoho National Park (~45 min E — closest big terrain)
| Hike | Dist | Gain | Difficulty | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ★★ **Lake O'Hara — Alpine Circuit** (Wiwaxy Gap → Oesa → Yukness Ledges → Opabin) | ~11–12 km loop | ~800–880 m | Difficult (scree, exposed ledges) | **The single best alpine day in the Rockies.** Hanging above turquoise Oesa & the Opabin Plateau under Victoria/Lefroy/Huber. Access = cancellation-monitor the bus or **hike the 11 km road in, bus out space-available** (a ~33 km day with the full circuit — very doable for you). |
| ★ **Iceline Trail** (+ Little Yoho / Twin Falls loop) | 13.6 km o&b · up to ~20–21 km loop | 945–1,090 m | Moderate (no exposure) | Walks the toe of the Emerald Glacier past meltwater tarns; Takakkaw Falls (Canada's 2nd-highest) at the trailhead. **The best no-reservation big day from Golden.** |

### Lake Louise / Moraine Lake (~1 h — the marquee summits)
| Hike | Dist | Gain | Difficulty | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ★★ **Mount Temple** (scramble) | ~16 km | ~1,690 m · summit 3,543 m | **Difficult scramble** — real exposure, 3 cliff bands, rockfall | The prize peak: a genuine "big mountain" without ropes, over Moraine Lake & the Ten Peaks. **Mid-July–mid-Sept only.** Take the 4/5 AM Alpine Start shuttle for a safe descent window. |
| ★ **Sentinel Pass via Larch Valley** | ~11.4 km | ~817 m | Moderate–hard | Valley of the Ten Peaks to a high pass; same Moraine trailhead as Temple. (Group-of-4 bear rule may apply.) |
| **Fairview Mountain** (summit) | ~10.6 km | ~1,014 m | Hard past Saddleback | The accessible "real summit" at Lake Louise — stare straight down 1,000 m onto the lake. |
| **Plain of Six Glaciers + Big Beehive + Devil's Thumb** | ~13–21 km | ~800–1,100 m | Moderate + short scramble | Two teahouses, a glacier amphitheatre, airy perches over both lakes. Best Lake Louise "sampler." |

### Kicking Horse Resort (in Golden — gondola-assisted summit day)
- **Golden Eagle Express gondola** to 2,347 m (~1,000 m free). Season May 30–Sep 27; daily to Sep 7, then **Thu–Mon**.
- **Terminator Peak (T1)** — signature ridge scramble off the top ("Most Difficult," 1–2 h). **Bowl Over Loop** for the biggest self-powered circuit up top; hike up via **Showdown** to earn the vert.
- ★ **Via Ferrata "Ascension"** — back in 2026, guided, one of North America's most technical VF routes, summits Terminator Peak (~$215). A standout half-day for this group.

|---|---|---|---|---|

| **Wilcox Pass** | ~8 km | ~335–520 m | Moderate | Fast climb to eye-level Athabasca Glacier / Columbia Icefield views + bighorn sheep. High reward-per-effort; ~2.5 h from Golden. |

|---|---|---|---|---|

### Banff town area
**Cory Pass Loop** (13 km / ~915 m / hard) — dramatic knife-edge pass under Mount Edith's fin, through the eerie Gargoyle Valley; **no reservation**, ideal weather-buffer day. · **Cascade Mountain** (~20 km / ~1,460 m, non-technical but long; exposed traverse near the false summit — late-July–Sept only; never descend through the amphitheatre).

### Optional side-trip · Jasper (2–3 nights within the block)
Golden→Jasper is **~4 h** — **too far for a day trip.** If you want the Icefields Parkway's north end and Jasper proper, fold in a **2–3-night side-base** mid-block, driving the Parkway up (Wilcox Pass, Parker Ridge, Cirque Peak en route). **2026 reality after the 2024 wildfire:** town is open and functioning, but **Mount Edith Cavell (Cavell Meadows / Path of the Glacier) is CLOSED all 2026** — a real loss. Still open & worth it: **Sulphur Skyline** (~8 km / ~700 m, 360° summit + Miette Hot Springs soak), **Bald Hills** (best Maligne Lake viewpoint), **Wilcox Pass**, and the **SkyTram** to The Whistlers (valley trailhead itself is closed). **Verdict:** worthwhile as a short side-base if you can spare 2–3 nights; otherwise just do **Wilcox Pass** at the Columbia Icefield end (much closer) and skip the townsite.

## Logistics

**Vehicle.** An AWD/SUV is plenty (no true off-roading needed; the Meadows-in-the-Sky and Yoho Valley roads are paved/gravel but fine). Golden→Vancouver return is the big mileage; budget fuel and never pass a station under half a tank on the Trans-Canada.

**Passes to buy up front.** Parks Canada **Discovery Pass** (annual — covers Banff/Yoho/Jasper/Glacier/Revelstoke for 26 days); **Kananaskis Conservation Pass** (Canmore peaks); day-of **Garibaldi** & **Joffre** passes as needed (7 AM PT, 2 days prior).

**Food.** Golden has full grocery (Save-On-Foods, IGA) — big-shop for the 14-night block. Field/Lake Louise are tiny and pricey. Kamloops and Squamish/Whistler restock the western leg. Pack trail lunches daily; teahouses (Lake Agnes, Plain of Six Glaciers) are the rare on-trail exceptions.

**Bears.** Grizzly & black bear country throughout — bear spray on the hip (not the pack), make noise, and honour the **group-of-4 rules** (Balu Pass; sometimes Larch Valley/Sentinel Pass). Never the Cascade amphitheatre descent.

**Safety.** Register intentions before big scrambles (Temple, Cirque, Black Tusk). Check **511 Alberta / DriveBC** for road status, park bulletins for fire/trail closures, and **BC Air Quality warnings** for smoke each morning. Early-Sept snow can glaze high scrambles — carry microspikes for Temple/Cirque late in the block.

**Weather-flex doctrine (same as Iceland).** Every base has a flex day; every summit a lighter swap partner. Rank days by forecast — summits on the clear, stable day; valley/glacier walks and teahouse loops when it's socked in; driving on the ugly day. Smoke is the wildcard — keep regional options open.
