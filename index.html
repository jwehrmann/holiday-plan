The Shape of the Trip

You land at KEF on Sept 10, 9:25 AM (YVR red-eye) and fly out Sept 29. The route runs clockwise: south coast glaciers → Eastfjords → the North → Snæfellsnes, finishing 2.5 h from the airport instead of 6. Two changes from your draft, both to cut dead driving: Egilsstaðir replaces Seyðisfjörður as the East base (it's the hub every East hike radiates from — Seyðisfjörður becomes a day trip over the pass), and the Eyjafjörður valley south of Akureyri replaces Siglufjörður in the North (Kerling's trailhead is 30 min away instead of 1 h 15, Aldeyjarfoss 1 h 15 instead of 2 h 30 — Siglufjörður becomes a day trip). Big landmarks are hit at sunrise or late evening; the crowd-free midday hours go to trails tour buses can't reach.

Base 01

South Coast & Glacier Realm

Sept 10 – 15 · 5 nights
🏠 Airbnb in Kirkjubæjarklaustur (or a rural cabin just east) — the pivot point between the waterfall coast and Vatnajökull's glacier tongues

Klaustur sits deliberately east of the tour-bus bubble. Icons like Skógafoss and Seljalandsfoss are a dawn strike west; Múlagljúfur, Skaftafell and Jökulsárlón are under an hour east. Stock groceries in Selfoss on arrival day — Klaustur has only a small Kjarval.

Off-beatSunrise

Múlagljúfur CanyonUnmarked trail to Hangandifoss — Iceland's best-kept canyon

🚗 55 km · 45 min E↔ 7.1 km RT⛰ 386 mModerate⏱ 2.5–3 h

Steep, muddy, unsigned — which is exactly why you'll likely have a moss-drenched mega-canyon with two plunging waterfalls to yourselves at dawn. Fog hangs in the gorge on cool September mornings; bring the 60 MP sensor and a polarizer.

Base 02

The Eastfjords

Sept 15 – 20 · 5 nights
🏠 Airbnb in/near Egilsstaðir — the East's road hub; every hike below radiates from it. (Prefer waking up to a fjord? Seyðisfjörður works too, at the cost of a 27 km mountain pass each way, ~+50 min/day.)

The least-visited quarter of the Ring Road. This is where the trip gets quiet: alpine boulder fields, columnar-basalt canyons, and fjord hikes where the only footprints are reindeer.

Off-beat

Stórurð — The Giant's BouldersTurquoise ponds among house-sized tuff blocks

🚗 ~45 km · 45 min (Vatnsskarð)↔ 10.8 km RT⛰ 607 mModerate+⏱ 4–4.5 h

Under the jagged Dyrfjöll "Door Mountains," a glacial valley strewn with colossal boulders and impossibly bright ponds. Save it for the best-weather day of the block — snow can arrive up here by late September. Poles help on the loose descent.

Stuðlagil Canyon — east bankBasalt columns over glacier-blue water

Base 03

Eyjafjörður & the North

Sept 20 – 25 · 5 nights
🏠 Airbnb/cabin in the Eyjafjörður valley near Akureyri — 30 min to Kerling's trailhead, 1 h 15 to Aldeyjarfoss, 30 min to Goðafoss, with Siglufjörður as a fjord-tunnel day trip. (Your draft's Siglufjörður is lovely but puts 1–2.5 h between you and every major hike.)

The North block is built around one monster summit, one perfect dark-basalt waterfall, and the volcanic weirdness of Mývatn — with a herring-town day and a genuine flex day, because by day 12 your legs will have opinions.

Summit day

Kerling (1,538 m)The highest peak in North Iceland — the trip's crux

🚗 ~25 km · 30 min↔ 12.2 km RT⛰ 1,416 mVery hard⏱ 7–8 h

1,400+ vertical metres of relentless volcanic rubble to the roof of the North — on a clear day you see Eyjafjörður end to end. Expect snow patches up top in late September; hard-shell, gloves, and a strict turnaround time. Total isolation. Total payoff.

AldeyjarfossWhite water through black basalt — your 4x4's reward

🚗 ~75 km · 1 h 15 (last stretch gravel/F26)↔ 2.4 km⛰ 77 mEasy walk⏱ 1 h

The Skjálfandafljót punches through a jet-black colonnade — the best micro-contrast subject in Iceland. The highland approach keeps 95% of visitors away; at sunrise, likely 100%. Pair with Goðafoss on the drive back and Hrafnabjargafoss 4 km further upstream if the road is good.

Base 04 · Recommended

Snæfellsnes Peninsula

Sept 25 – 29 · 4 nights
🏠 Airbnb in Grundarfjörður — Kirkjufell is 2 km from your door; the whole national park is within 50 min; KEF is a safe 2.5 h on departure morning

Why Snæfellsnes over your draft's Westfjords: it swaps ~6 hours of extra transfer driving (including a risky 6-hour airport haul on flight day) for more time on trails, and still delivers a hard unmarked summit, sea cliffs, lava fields, a glacier-topped volcano — with a fraction of the south coast's crowds in late September. The full Westfjords version is preserved below if the remote pull wins.

🚗 2 km · 5 min↔ 3.5 km⛰ 16 mEasy⏱ 1 h

Living 5 minutes away is the cheat code: walk over for sunrise before any tour van leaves Reykjavík, and come back on any aurora night — the arrowhead peak under green sky is the classic for a reason. Waterfall-foreground comp works best with the tide-fed pools still.

Alternative Base 04

The Westfjords Version

Sept 25 – 29 · Ísafjörður
The trade, honestly: the Westfjords are Iceland's emptiest, most dramatic corner — deeper solitude than anything on the main route. The cost: the Sept 25 transfer grows to ~5.5–6 h (vs 4 h with a hike included), roads are slower and part gravel, weather cancellations bite harder with no nearby substitutes, and departure day becomes a ~6 h drive to KEF before your flight. Pick this if raw remoteness outranks trail time and airport safety margin.
Sunset

DynjandiThe bridal-veil giant of the fjords

🚗 ~60 km · 1 h from Ísafjörður↔ 1.6 km⛰ 92 mShort + steep⏱ 40 min

100 m wide at its base, seven falls stacked beneath it, facing west into the sunset over Arnarfjörður. The climb past the lower falls layers composition after composition.

Provisions, Fuel & Survival Logistics

🛒 Food strategy

  • Rule: big-shop at the cheap chains (Bónus, Krónan, Nettó) at each base handover; rural stores are sparse and pricey.
  • Day 1: Krónan/Bónus Selfoss — 5 days of food before Klaustur (only a small Kjarval there).
  • Sept 15: Nettó/Bónus Egilsstaðir.
  • Sept 20/21: Bónus Akureyri (biggest selection outside Reykjavík).
  • Sept 25: Bónus Borgarnes (Snæfellsnes) or Bónus Ísafjörður (Westfjords) — last big shops.
  • Hike food: skyr, flatkökur, dried fish, chocolate, instant soup. Pack lunch every day — trailhead cafés basically don't exist.

⛽ Fuel discipline

  • Rule of thumb: never below half a tank. Gaps between stations in the East and Westfjords exceed 100 km.
  • Get an N1 key fob / Orkan app — most rural pumps are unmanned, card-only, and some reject foreign cards without a PIN.
  • Critical fill-ups: Selfoss → Vík → Klaustur → Höfn → Egilsstaðir → Reykjahlíð (Mývatn) → Akureyri → Borgarnes (or Hólmavík/Ísafjörður).
  • Budget: ~2,900 km × ~9 L/100km 4x4 ≈ 260 L ≈ 80,000 ISK (~$570).

💧 Water & kit

  • Tap water is glacier-grade everywhere. Carry two 1.5 L bottles each, refill daily; a thermos of hot drink earns its weight on summit days. (Ignore the sulfur smell in hot taps — cold is pristine.)
  • Streams above farms are drinkable on trail; glacial (milky) water is not.
  • Layers: hard-shell top+bottom, insulation, hat/gloves — Kerling and Helgrindur can be winter up top in late Sept.
  • Microspikes (light ones) for early-morning frost near falls; headlamps for pre-dawn starts.
  • Offline: download Iceland in Google Maps + AllTrails/Wikiloc tracks; cell coverage dies in exactly the places you're going.

🚙 Driving & safety

  • 4x4 needed for: Þakgil road, Aldeyjarfoss (F26 southern stretch), Stuðlagil east parking gravel, Kvennaskarð (Westfjords option).
  • Check road.is + vedur.is every morning; safetravel.is trip plan before Kerling/Helgrindur/Kaldbakur.
  • September = first storms. Hold doors against wind gusts (the classic rental damage) and never drive in a yellow/red weather warning.
  • Headlights on 24/7 (law). Sheep own the road.
🌋 Volcano watch (as of July 2026): no eruption is active right now — the last Sundhnúkur (Reykjanes) eruption ended in Aug 2025 — but magma is re-accumulating under Svartsengi and the Met Office rates a new eruption as likely. The whole eruption zone sits 20 min from KEF, far from every base in this plan, so it's zero risk to the itinerary — and if a fissure opens while you're here, it's a once-in-a-lifetime bolt-on for arrival or departure day. Light & Sky Calendar

🌅 Daylight windows

  • Sept 10 (Klaustur): sunrise 6:22, sunset 19:59 — 13.6 h of light.
  • Sept 29 (Grundarfjörður): sunrise 7:37, sunset 19:12 — 11.6 h. You lose ~6 min/day; full table below.
  • Golden hour is long and lazy at this latitude — roughly 1.5 h after sunrise and before sunset.
  • Every "sunrise strike" in this plan means arriving at the trailhead/falls ~30 min before the listed sunrise.

🌌 Aurora playbook

  • New moon Sept 11, full moon Sept 26 — darkest skies land in Bases 1–2; late-trip nights are moonlit (still shootable, different look).
  • Nightly ritual: check the vedur.is aurora forecast (Kp + cloud map) at ~21:00; chase gaps in cloud, not Kp numbers.
  • Pre-scouted foregrounds: Jökulsárlón (B1), Stuðlagil rim / Seyðisfjörður (B2), Goðafoss & Mývatn (B3), Kirkjufell & Búðakirkja (B4).
  • Settings baseline: 14–20 mm, f/1.8–2.8, 5–10 s, ISO 1600–6400 (drop ISO under moonlight).

📷 a7RV field notes

  • Waterfall spray is relentless: 3+ microfiber cloths, a rocket blower, and a rain cover; wipe between every frame at Skógafoss/Dettifoss.
  • Polarizer for moss/canyon work (Múlagljúfur, Fjaðrárgljúfur); 6-stop ND for silky water at Aldeyjarfoss/Goðafoss.
  • Cold kills batteries ~30% faster — keep spares in an inside pocket; 60 MP files eat cards, bring doubles.
  • Crowd-dodging summary: icons at dawn or after 18:00; unmarked trails at midday; nobody is anywhere before 8 AM in September.

How to read it: Bases 1–2 are your true dark-sky window — a thin or absent moon means maximum aurora contrast and Milky Way frames (Jökulsárlón, Stuðlagil, Seyðisfjörður). From Base 3 on, the waxing moon lights the landscape itself: aurora still shows on camera, but switch strategy to moonlit foregrounds — glaciers, waterfalls and Kirkjufell rendered in silver instead of silhouette. Lower ISO to 400–800 and let the moon do the light painting.
Weather-flex doctrine: every base has one designated flex day and every hard hike a swap partner. Rank days by forecast each evening (vedur.is), not by this schedule — the plan is a menu, the sky is the boss. If a summit day (Kristínartindar, Kerling, Helgrindur) has no visibility window in a block, trade it for canyons and waterfalls without regret: they're better in brooding weather anyway.
