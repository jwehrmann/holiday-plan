The Shape of the Trip

You land at KEF on Sept 10, 9:25 AM (YVR red-eye) and fly out Sept 29. The route runs clockwise: South Coast → Skaftafell → Eastfjords → North → then back southwest to Snæfellsnes → a gentle Reykjanes finish beside KEF. Svínafell unlocks Kristínartindar; Egilsstaðir is the East road hub; Akureyri provides Súlur and the last city-scale resupply; Arnarstapi drops you in the middle of the Snæfellsnes hikes; Reykjanes ends the trip 10 min from the airport. Within each base the dates are labels, not promises: choose days by weather, road status and fatigue. Bases 1–5 are booked; Base 6 remains to book. Click a hike card for its concise field reference.

Provisions, Fuel & Survival Logistics

🛒 Food strategy

  • Rule: big-shop at the cheap chains (Bónus, Krónan, Nettó) at each base handover; rural stores are sparse and pricey.
  • Day 1: Krónan Selfoss — stock the Hella cottage after the flight.
  • Sept 15: top-up at Krónan Vík or Kjarval Klaustur before Skaftafell — no store at Svínafell.
  • Sept 17: Nettó/Bónus Egilsstaðir on the way into Base 3.
  • Sept 19/20: Bónus Akureyri (biggest selection outside Reykjavík).
  • Sept 23: big-shop Bónus/Krónan Borgarnes on the way to Snæfellsnes — Arnarstapi has no store (Ólafsvík ~30 min).
  • Hike food: skyr, flatkökur, dried fish, chocolate, instant soup. Pack lunch every day — trailhead cafés basically don't exist.

⛽ Fuel discipline

  • Rule of thumb: never below half a tank. Gaps between stations in the East and Westfjords exceed 100 km.
  • Get an N1 key fob / Orkan app — most rural pumps are unmanned, card-only, and some reject foreign cards without a PIN.
  • Critical fill-ups: Selfoss → Vík → Klaustur → Höfn → Egilsstaðir → Reykjahlíð (Mývatn) → Akureyri → Blönduós → Borgarnes → Ólafsvík → (Sept 27) Borgarnes → Keflavík.
  • Planning quantity: ~3,200 km at 9 L/100 km is roughly 290 L. Reprice it against the actual rental and pump prices shortly before departure.

💧 Water & kit

  • Tap water is glacier-grade everywhere. Carry two 1.5 L bottles each, refill daily; a thermos of hot drink earns its weight on summit days. (Ignore the sulfur smell in hot taps — cold is pristine.)
  • Do not assume surface water is safe. Refill treated tap water; filter or treat any emergency stream water. Never drink silty glacial water.
  • Layers: hard-shell top+bottom, insulation, hat/gloves — Súlur, Stórurð and Helgrindur can be wintry in late September.
  • Microspikes (light ones) for early-morning frost near falls; headlamps for pre-dawn starts.
  • Offline: download Iceland in Google Maps + AllTrails/Wikiloc tracks; cell coverage dies in exactly the places you're going.

🚙 Driving & safety

  • Rental permission matters as much as traction: verify F-road coverage for Landmannalaugar and Aldeyjarfoss. Kvennaskarð needs a well-equipped jeep and local approval; an ordinary crossover badge is not enough.
  • Check umferdin.is/road.is + vedur.is every morning; file safetravel.is plans for Súlur, Stórurð and any remote summit.
  • September = first storms. Hold doors against wind gusts (the classic rental damage) and never drive in a yellow/red weather warning.
  • Headlights on 24/7 (law). Sheep own the road.

2 · Skaftafell / Öræfi (Sept 15–17)~9–10°C / 4–5°C~110–140 mm · 14–17 daysGlacier-cool; Öræfi sits in Vatnajökull's variable rain shadow — showers with big clearings and katabatic gusts off the icecap. Kristínartindar's top is exposed: wind is the summit go/no-go.
3 · Eastfjords (Sept 17–19)~9–10°C / 3–5°C~90–100 mm · 12–15 daysOften the driest quarter in westerly flow — Egilsstaðir sits in a rain shadow and gets Iceland's best föhn-warmed afternoons. Stórurð (600 m+) can see its first snow dusting late Sept.
4 · North (Sept 19–23)~8–9°C / 2–4°C~55–75 mm · the fewest rain daysDriest region, coldest nights. Súlur can carry snow or ice; Kerling remains guide-only regardless of forecast.
5 · Snæfellsnes (Sept 23–27)~9–10°C / 5–6°C~90–120 mm · 13–16 daysMilder and less exposed than the Westfjords, but the peninsula still makes its own wind — Helgrindur / Snæfellsjökull need a calm clear window, while the coast + NP loop work in most weather. The Fróðárheiði / Vatnaleið passes can catch the first snow.
6 · Reykjanes (Sept 27–29)~9–10°C / 5–6°C~90 mm · variableWindy, low-lying volcanic coast; two easy days where weather barely matters — the point is being 10 min from KEF. The wildcard here is volcanic activity, not rain.
How to run it in practice: every evening check vedur.is (text forecast + wind map) and belgingur.is; rank the next 3 days and slot hikes accordingly — summits on the calm day, canyons and waterfalls in the drizzle, driving on the ugly day. Yellow warning = reshuffle; orange/red = nobody drives. Rain here is usually sideways drizzle in 10–15 m/s wind rather than downpours: full hard-shell armor beats an umbrella (which Iceland eats for breakfast). And the classic September pattern — 4 grey days, then one impossibly clear one — is exactly why every base has a flex day.

Light & Sky Calendar

🌅 Daylight windows

  • Sept 10 (South Iceland): roughly 06:20 sunrise and 20:00 sunset — 13.6 h of light.
  • Sept 29 (Keflavík): sunrise 7:34, sunset 19:10 — 11.6 h. You lose ~6 min/day; full table below.
  • Golden hour is long and lazy at this latitude — roughly 1.5 h after sunrise and before sunset.
  • Every "sunrise strike" in this plan means arriving at the trailhead/falls ~30 min before the listed sunrise.

🌌 Aurora playbook

  • New moon Sept 11, full moon Sept 26 — darkest skies land in Bases 1–3; late-trip nights are moonlit (still shootable, different look).
  • Nightly ritual: check the vedur.is aurora forecast (Kp + cloud map) at ~21:00; chase gaps in cloud, not Kp numbers.
  • Pre-scouted foregrounds: Jökulsárlón / Diamond Beach (B2), Stuðlagil rim / Seyðisfjörður (B3), Goðafoss & Mývatn (B4), Kirkjufell + Búðakirkja + the hot tub (B5), Garður lighthouse (Sept 28–29). Full moon Sept 26 = moonlit foregrounds, not pitch black.
  • Settings baseline: 14–20 mm, f/1.8–2.8, 5–10 s, ISO 1600–6400 (drop ISO under moonlight).

📷 a7RV field notes

  • Waterfall spray is relentless: 3+ microfiber cloths, a rocket blower, and a rain cover; wipe between every frame at Skógafoss/Dettifoss.
  • Polarizer for moss/canyon work (Múlagljúfur, Fjaðrárgljúfur); 6-stop ND for silky water at Aldeyjarfoss/Goðafoss.
  • Cold kills batteries ~30% faster — keep spares in an inside pocket; 60 MP files eat cards, bring doubles.
  • Crowd-dodging summary: icons at dawn or after 18:00; unmarked trails at midday; nobody is anywhere before 8 AM in September.

Lower ISO to 400–800 and let the moon do the light painting.
Weather-flex doctrine: rank days each evening, recheck in the morning, and treat the tabs as a menu. Kristínartindar swaps to the lower Skaftafell loop; Stórurð swaps to Brúnavík; Súlur swaps to the Akureyri reset; Helgrindur swaps to the Snæfellsnes coast loop. Access-dependent roads never become mandatory just because a date is printed here.
